# APA 7th Edition Reference Rules (English)

> Source: Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association, 7th Edition (2019)

---

## 1. In-Text Citations

### 1.1 Basic Formats

| Authors | Parenthetical | Narrative |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| 1 author | (Smith, 2020) | Smith (2020) |
| 2 authors | (Smith & Jones, 2020) | Smith and Jones (2020) |
| 3+ authors | (Smith et al., 2020) | Smith et al. (2020) |
| Group/institution (first cite) | (American Psychological Association [APA], 2020) | American Psychological Association (APA, 2020) |
| Group/institution (subsequent) | (APA, 2020) | APA (2020) |
| No author | ("Article Title," 2020) or (Book Title, 2020) | — |
| No date | (Smith, n.d.) | Smith (n.d.) |

### 1.2 Multiple Works in One Citation
- Same-author works: list chronologically — (Smith, 2019, 2020)
- Different-author works: list alphabetically, separated by semicolons — (Brown, 2019; Smith & Jones, 2020)

### 1.3 Repeated Citations
- When the author's name appears in the narrative, the year may be omitted in repeated citations within the same paragraph if the source remains clear.
- If a paraphrase continues for several sentences, cite the work on first mention; no need to repeat if the context remains clear.

### 1.4 Direct Quotations
- Under 40 words: incorporate into text with quotation marks, include page number.
  - Parenthetical: (Smith, 2020, p. 45) or (Smith, 2020, pp. 45–46)
  - Narrative: Smith (2020) stated "..." (p. 45)
- 40 words or more: use a block quotation (indented 0.5 inch), no quotation marks, citation after final punctuation.

### 1.5 Secondary Sources
- Cite the secondary source; reference only the secondary source in the reference list.
- Format: (as cited in Smith, 2020)

## 2. Reference List: General Principles

### 2.1 Four Elements of Every Reference

```
Author. (Date). Title. Source.
```

| Element | Answers |
|---------|---------|
| Author | Who created the work? |
| Date | When was it published? |
| Title | What is the work called? |
| Source | Where can it be retrieved? |

### 2.2 Reference List Formatting
- Arrange entries alphabetically by author's last name (or by title if no author).
- Use a hanging indent (first line flush left, subsequent lines indented 0.5 inch).
- Double-space all entries.
- Up to 20 authors listed before ellipsis; list first 19, then ellipsis (…), then final author.

### 2.3 Author Format
- Invert all authors' names: Last, F. M.
- 2–20 authors: list all; separate with commas; use "&" before the final author.
- More than 20 authors: first 19, ellipsis, last author.
- Group/organization authors: spell out fully; do not abbreviate.
- No author: move title to author position.
- Editor in place of author: Ed. (single) or Eds. (multiple) in parentheses after the name.

### 2.4 Date Format
- Journal articles, books: year only — (2020).
- Magazines, newsletters, newspapers: year and full date — (2020, March 15).
- No date: (n.d.).
- In press: (in press).
- Original work republished: (Original work published 1950) at end of entry; in-text: (Author, 1950/2020).

## 3. Reference Formats by Type

### 3.1 Journal Article

```
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Article title in sentence case. Journal Title in Title Case, Volume(Issue), pp–pp. https://doi.org/xxxxx
```

**Rules:**
- Article title: sentence case (capitalize only the first word, proper nouns, and the first word after a colon).
- ⚠️ Publishers and databases (Google Scholar, PsycINFO, PubMed) often display article titles in Title Case. APA 7th requires sentence case — do NOT copy-paste the publisher's capitalization.
- Journal title and volume number: **italicize**.
- Issue number: in parentheses, not italicized.
- Page range: en dash, not hyphen.
- Include DOI as hyperlink (no "DOI:" label).

**Example:**
```
Bandura, A., & Walters, R. H. (1959). Adolescent aggression. Psychological Bulletin, 56(3), 234–250. https://doi.org/10.1037/0000000
```

### 3.2 Book

```
Author, A. A. (Year). Book title in sentence case (edition if applicable). Publisher.
```

**Rules:**
- Book title: **italicize**; sentence case.
- Edition information in parentheses after title, not italicized: (2nd ed.), (Rev. ed.).
- No publisher location needed (removed in 7th edition).

**Example:**
```
Strunk, W., Jr., & White, E. B. (2000). The elements of style (4th ed.). Longman.
```

### 3.3 Edited Book Chapter

```
Author, A. A. (Year). Chapter title in sentence case. In E. E. Editor (Ed.), Book title in sentence case (pp. xx–xx). Publisher. https://doi.org/xxxxx
```

**Rules:**
- Editor: name NOT inverted in edited book position — First M. Last (Ed.) or initials: F. M. Last (Ed.).
- Chapter title: sentence case, no italics.
- Book title: italics.
- Include page range with pp.

**Example:**
```
Guthrie, J. T., & Wigfield, A. (2000). Engagement and motivation in reading. In M. L. Kamil, P. B. Mosenthal, P. D. Pearson, & R. Barr (Eds.), Handbook of reading research (Vol. 3, pp. 403–422). Erlbaum.
```

### 3.4 Dissertation or Thesis

```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of dissertation in sentence case [Doctoral dissertation/Master's thesis, Institution Name]. Database Name. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Harris, L. (2014). Instructional leadership perceptions and practices of elementary school leaders [Unpublished doctoral dissertation]. University of Virginia.
```

### 3.5 Magazine Article

```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Article title. Magazine Title, Volume(Issue), pp–pp. URL
```

### 3.6 Newspaper Article

```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Article title. Newspaper Title. URL
```

### 3.7 Webpage / Website

```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Page title. Site Name. URL
```

- If no individual author, use the organization name as author.
- Include retrieval date only if content is likely to change: "Retrieved Month Day, Year, from URL"

### 3.8 Report

```
Author, A. A. (Year). Report title (Report No. xxx). Publisher. URL
```

### 3.9 Conference Paper / Presentation

```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day–Day). Paper title [Paper presentation]. Conference Name, City, Country. URL
```

## 4. DOI and URL Rules

- Present DOIs as hyperlinks: https://doi.org/xxxxx
- The label "DOI:" is no longer used.
- Use "Retrieved from" only when a retrieval date is also needed.
- Provide URLs for works without DOIs that are retrieved from the open web.
- Do not include database name (e.g., PsycINFO, EBSCO) for works retrieved from most academic databases.

---

## 5. Capitalization Rules

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Article / chapter title | Sentence case: capitalize first word, proper nouns, first word after colon |
| Book title | Sentence case; italicize |
| Journal title | Title Case (every major word capitalized); italicize |
| Heading in manuscript | See heading levels below |

---

## 6. Italics Usage

| Italicize | Do NOT italicize |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Journal title | Article title |
| Volume number of journal | Issue number |
| Book title | Edition info in parentheses |
| Film, TV show, album titles | Chapter title |
| Test name (e.g., Beck Depression Inventory) | — |

---

## 7. Heading Levels (APA 7th)

| Level | Format |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | **Bold, Title Case, Centered** |
| 2 | **Bold, Title Case, Left-Aligned** |
| 3 | **Bold, Italic, Title Case, Left-Aligned** |
| 4 | **Bold, Title Case, Indented, ends with period.** Paragraph continues. |
| 5 | **Bold, Italic, Title Case, Indented, ends with period.** Paragraph continues. |

---

## 8. Numbers

- Spell out numbers below 10 in general text; use numerals for 10 and above.
- Use numerals for exact measurements, statistics, scores, and numbers in lists/tables.
- Do not spell out numbers in abstracts.
- Use numerals for ages, times, dates, participants, and scores regardless of size.

---

## 9. Statistical Reporting

- Italicize statistical symbols: *M*, *SD*, *t*, *F*, *p*, *r*, *N*, *n*, *df*
- Report exact *p* values to two or three decimal places; if p < .001, write *p* < .001.
- No zero before the decimal for values that cannot exceed 1.0 (e.g., *p* = .043, *r* = .32). Include a leading zero for values that CAN exceed 1.0 (e.g., *t* = 0.43, *F* = 0.89, *M* = 0.56).
- Use en dash (–) for value ranges, not hyphen.
